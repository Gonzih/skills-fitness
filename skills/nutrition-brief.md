Write a clear, practical nutrition guidance brief based on the client details below. Keep recommendations evidence-based, realistic, and easy to follow.

## Nutrition Guidance Brief

### Client Overview
- Name / identifier
- Goal (fat loss, muscle gain, performance, general health)
- Current dietary habits (summary)
- Food preferences, dislikes, or cultural considerations
- Allergies or intolerances

### Calorie & Macronutrient Targets
- Daily calorie target (with brief rationale)
- Protein target (grams and % of calories)
- Carbohydrate target (grams and % of calories)
- Fat target (grams and % of calories)
- Fiber target

### Meal Timing Guidelines
- Recommended meal frequency
- Pre-workout nutrition (timing, composition)
- Post-workout nutrition (timing, composition)
- Guidance around sleep / overnight fasting

### Food Recommendations

#### Prioritize (High-Quality Sources)
- Proteins: (examples)
- Carbohydrates: (examples)
- Fats: (examples)
- Vegetables & Fiber: (examples)

#### Limit or Moderate
- Foods to reduce and why
- Approximate frequency or portion guidance

#### Avoid (if applicable)
- Foods incompatible with goals or restrictions

### Hydration
- Daily water intake target
- Electrolyte considerations (especially around training)

### Supplement Suggestions (if relevant)
- Evidence-based supplements relevant to the client's goal
- Dosage and timing notes
- Note: supplements are secondary to whole-food nutrition

### Sample Day of Eating
Provide a realistic sample meal plan showing how the targets can be met:
- Breakfast
- Mid-morning snack (if applicable)
- Lunch
- Pre/post-workout snack
- Dinner
- Evening snack (if applicable)
- Approximate macros and calories per meal

### Key Habits to Build
- 2–4 simple, high-impact nutrition habits to focus on first
- Prioritize consistency over perfection

### Progress Check Points
- What to track (food log, weight, energy, performance)
- When to reassess and adjust targets

## Live Data Sources

- **Open Food Facts API** (world.openfoodfacts.org/data): Look up nutritional data for specific foods and branded products to provide accurate macro breakdowns and ingredient transparency.
- **USDA FoodData Central API** (fdc.nal.usda.gov): Access comprehensive nutritional profiles for whole foods and ingredients to build accurate sample meal plans and validate macro targets.
- **NIH Dietary Reference Intakes** (ods.od.nih.gov): Reference the National Institutes of Health's dietary reference intake values for vitamins, minerals, and macronutrients to ensure recommendations meet established nutritional standards.
